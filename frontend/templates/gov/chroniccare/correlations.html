Food Environment & Disease Correlations

MAHA Thesis: There is a strong correlation between food environment factors (fast food density, food desert status, SNAP participation) and chronic disease prevalence.
Key Correlations
Factor Disease Correlation
Fast Food Density Obesity +0.72
Food Desert Status Diabetes +0.68
Grocery Store Access Heart Disease -0.54
SNAP Participation Obesity +0.45

Policy Implications
Evidence Supports
  • Increasing grocery store access in food deserts
  • Limiting fast food density near schools
  • SNAP incentives for healthy foods
Recommended Actions
  • Target high-correlation counties for intervention
  • Partner with grocery chains for expansion
  • Reform SNAP eligible products list
Expected Impact
  • 5-10% reduction in obesity in targeted areas
  • $2.3B Medicare savings over 10 years
  • Improved health equity scores
