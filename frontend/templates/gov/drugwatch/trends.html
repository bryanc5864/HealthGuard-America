Key Insights
  • The top 10 drugs account for approximately 30% of total Medicare Part D spending
  • Brand-name drugs dominate spending despite generics being available for many conditions
  • Specialty drugs (biologics, cancer treatments) have the highest per-unit costs
  • Diabetes medications represent a significant portion of total spending
  • GLP-1 agonists (Ozempic, Mounjaro) are the fastest-growing spending category

Policy Implications
IRA Drug Negotiation

The Inflation Reduction Act allows Medicare to negotiate prices for select high-spend drugs. First 10 drugs selected in 2023 represent $50B+ in annual spending.

Price Cap Opportunity

Annual price increases now capped at inflation rate. Manufacturers exceeding cap must pay rebates to Medicare.

Out-of-Pocket Cap

New $2,000 annual out-of-pocket cap for Part D beneficiaries starting 2025 will shift costs to plans and manufacturers.
