What is MFN Pricing?

Most Favored Nation (MFN) pricing is a policy where the US would pay no more for drugs than the lowest price paid by other developed nations. Currently, Americans pay 2-3x more for the same medications compared to Canada, Australia, and the UK.

This analysis compares Medicare Part D spending against international reference prices to estimate potential savings if MFN pricing were implemented.

Benefits of MFN
  • Reduce Medicare spending by ~$180B annually
  • Lower out-of-pocket costs for seniors
  • Reduce Part D premium growth
  • Improve medication adherence (lower costs = better compliance)
  • Level playing field with other developed nations
Considerations
  • Potential impact on pharmaceutical R&D investment
  • Implementation complexity with existing contracts
  • Industry lobbying and political resistance
  • International reference pricing methodology debates
  • Phase-in period requirements
Methodology

MFN savings estimates are calculated by comparing US Medicare Part D prices to the lowest available price among reference countries (Canada, Australia, UK). The 65% average reduction is based on published studies comparing drug prices across OECD nations. Actual savings would depend on implementation details, drug selection, and negotiation outcomes.
