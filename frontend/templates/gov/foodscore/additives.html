About Additive Risk Scoring:

Food additives are substances added to foods during processing. While many are safe, some have been linked to health concerns including hyperactivity in children, allergic reactions, and potential carcinogenic effects. Our ML-powered Additive Scorer (85.71% accuracy) analyzes additives and assigns risk scores based on scientific literature, regulatory status across countries, and known health effects.

Policy Considerations

Consider Banning

  • Artificial colors (especially Red 40, Yellow 5, Yellow 6)
  • BHA and BHT preservatives
  • Brominated vegetable oil (BVO)
  • Potassium bromate

Require Warning Labels

  • Artificial sweeteners
  • MSG and related glutamates
  • Sodium nitrite/nitrate
  • Carrageenan

Align with EU Standards

  • EU bans 1,300+ additives allowed in US
  • Titanium dioxide (E171) - banned in EU 2022
  • Many artificial colors require warning labels in EU

Risk Scoring Methodology

Additive risk scores (0-100) are calculated based on: regulatory status across countries, published toxicology studies, known allergenic potential, carcinogenicity classifications (IARC), and reported adverse effects. Scores above 70 indicate additives with documented health concerns. This tool is for policy analysis and should not replace professional medical or regulatory advice.
