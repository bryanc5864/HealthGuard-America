About This Analysis:

SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program) provides nutrition assistance to low-income individuals and families. This analysis examines the health quality of foods currently eligible for purchase with SNAP benefits using the NOVA food classification system and MAHA health scores.

Policy Recommendations

Consider Removing

  • Sugary sodas and energy drinks
  • Candy and confectionery
  • Products with MAHA score < 25
  • Items with > 10 flagged additives

Consider Limiting

  • NOVA 4 ultra-processed foods
  • High-sodium snack foods
  • Sweetened breakfast cereals
  • Frozen prepared meals

Incentivize

  • Fresh fruits and vegetables
  • NOVA 1 unprocessed foods
  • Whole grains and legumes
  • Products with MAHA score > 70

Methodology

This analysis uses the NOVA food classification system (Groups 1-4) and MAHA health scores (0-100) derived from nutritional content, additive presence, and processing level. Products are analyzed from the Open Food Facts database, filtered for US market availability. MAHA scores below 40 are considered high-risk. Policy recommendations are based on public health research linking ultra-processed food consumption to adverse health outcomes.
